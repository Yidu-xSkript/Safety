# Porn Blocker + Accountability ("Witness") Platforms — Research

**Date:** 2026-06-30

## What you asked for

1. **A blocker** — blocks porn sites outright.
2. **Accountability reporting** — if you try to access blocked content, it reports to a person you designate (your "witness" / accountability partner).
3. **Search-history visibility for the witness** — including activity in a **private / incognito window**.

The category you want is called **accountability + filtering software**. Three platforms cover all three of your needs. Below is the honest version.

---

## Top recommendation: **Covenant Eyes**

The most established name in this space (faith-based origin, but works for anyone). It does **both** jobs you described — filtering *and* accountability — and its design sidesteps the incognito problem.

**How it handles your three requirements:**
- **Blocker** — Two filter levels. "Standard" blocks adult sites and forces SafeSearch. "Strong" also blocks VPN/proxy domains and mixed-content sites (e.g. Reddit) so you can't tunnel around it.
- **Reports to your witness** — You add an "ally." They get reports of flagged activity (time, frequency, context).
- **Incognito coverage** — This is the key strength: Covenant Eyes works by taking **periodic screenshots of your screen**, not by reading browser history. AI blurs the images and flags explicit content. Because it captures the *screen*, **incognito/private mode is irrelevant — it sees whatever you see.**

**Price:** $16.99/month Premium (or ~$183.99/year, up to 10 users, unlimited devices).

**The one honest catch:** Covenant Eyes' filtering can be bypassed depending on setup, and its real power is the *accountability* half — the screenshots. It's primarily an "I-know-someone-will-see-this" deterrent, backed by a blocker. That only works if your witness is a real, engaged human who actually reads the reports.

## Strong alternative: **Ever Accountable**

Closest match to your exact wording ("report when I try to access it," "even in a private window").

- **Blocker** — built-in porn filter/blocker.
- **Reports to your witness** — partner gets your internet/app activity and real-time alerts when explicit content is viewed.
- **Incognito coverage** — explicitly advertises that it **captures incognito browsing** (system/VPN-level monitoring, not just browser history). This is exactly the "even through a private window" requirement.
- ISO-certified (the only one in the category), which matters because this software sees everything you do.

**Price:** 14-day free trial, then $14.99/month or $129/year, unlimited devices.

## Bottom line for you

**Pick Covenant Eyes** if you want one tool that both blocks and reports, with incognito automatically covered by its screenshot design. **Pick Ever Accountable** if you want the most literal match to "block it, and tell my witness even if I go private" with a built-in real-time alert.

Either way, the software is only the scaffolding. At 15 years deep, the **witness** is the active ingredient — pick someone you'd genuinely hate to disappoint, who will actually open the reports and follow up with you. The blocker buys you the 10 seconds of friction; the witness is what makes you not want to spend them.

## What makes them the best

- **They combine filtering + accountability.** Most blockers you can uninstall in 30 seconds; the accountability layer means uninstalling *itself* gets reported to your witness.
- **They solve the incognito hole** — the #1 way people defeat ordinary blockers — either by screen-level screenshots (Covenant Eyes, Truple) or system/VPN-level monitoring (Ever Accountable, Accountable2You).
- **Cross-platform** (Windows, Mac, Android, iOS).
- **Designed around a human relationship**, not just a wall — which is what actually drives long-term change.

## What makes them bad / weak points

- **iOS is the weak link.** Apple restricts what apps can monitor. On iPhone these tools lean on a VPN profile + Screen Time, which a determined user can disable. Lock Screen Time with a passcode your witness holds.
- **Screenshot accountability = a privacy tradeoff.** Covenant Eyes/Truple periodically photograph your whole screen — banking, messages, everything (blurred, but still). You're trading privacy for accountability on purpose; just go in knowing it.
- **Reports are after-the-fact.** Accountability tools deter, they don't physically stop the moment. Covenant Eyes' *blocker* helps, but no filter is 100%.
- **Bypassable by a technical user** — secondary devices, work computers, a clean phone, factory reset. The system assumes you *want* to be caught.
- **Dead weight without an engaged witness.** Every source agrees: if the partner never reads the reports, the whole thing collapses into theater.
- **Ongoing cost** (~$130–180/yr).

## What could be improved (and how to improve YOUR setup)

Improvements the products themselves still lack:
- **Tamper/uninstall instantly alerts the witness** (some do this; insist on it being on).
- **True system-wide DNS/VPN blocking** that can't be killed without the witness's password.
- **Better iOS depth** — held back by Apple, not the vendors.
- **Real-time *blocking* of newly-flagged content**, not just delayed reporting.

How to make your own setup near bulletproof:
1. **Two layers:** run the blocker **plus** accountability (Covenant Eyes does both; or pair a DNS filter with Ever Accountable).
2. **Install on *every* device** you own — phone, laptop, tablet, work machine. The gap is where relapse lives.
3. **Give the witness the admin/uninstall password**, not you. You should not be able to turn it off alone.
4. **Lock iOS Screen Time** with a passcode only the witness knows.
5. **Add network-level DNS filtering** (e.g. a filtering DNS on your home router) so even an unprotected device on your Wi-Fi is covered.
6. **Pick the right witness** — engaged, unshockable, will actually call you. This single choice matters more than which app you buy.
7. **Pair it with real support** — at 15 years this is a genuine compulsive pattern; software + a counselor/group (e.g. SAA, or a therapist who does CBT/ACT for compulsive behavior) beats software alone. You're clearly already fighting for this — back the tool with people.
